Welcome to Preaching Life Change Ministry

Institutional capacity, structures, Size and coverage:
With a congregation of approx. 400 adults and over 500 Sunday school children and teenagers, the church provides spiritual nourishment to residents of Milima,kamukuywa, Maliki,Sikhendu, Chalicha Musharake, Kiminini the larger Kiminini Sub-county and other Trans nzoia and Bungoma who worship at the Sikhendu Church.

Our Mission

To be a church called by Jesus Christ to demonstrate his love to our community and around the world, to all people regardless of religion, race and background. By teaching and reaching out to our community by impacting and changing positively spiritual, social and economic life of every humanbeing. Our goal is to make sure that every soul is saved before the coming of our lord Jesus Christ.

About

Who we are

Church History and Background
Preaching life change ministry Church was started and founded by Bishop Dr John Njoroge Mburu.The Church was started and founded by Bishop Dr. John Njoroge Mburu in 2016 at the mother church in Sikhendu near Chalicha junction with 25 members. It has since grown into a big church with an approximate population of 600 dedicated members. The future plan is to become an ininternational ministry with over 2500 churches. Bishop Dr John Njoroge the senior pastor at mother church, has a vision of holistically reaching all the residents of Trans Nzoia county with the gospel of Jesus Christ. The church is currently striving to build a center that will serve as “the center of light” for the entire population of Kiminini constituency through spiritual healing, community resource, vocational training, education, feeding, HIV/AIDS response and community health.
Leadership and management
Under the support of the founding father, the Bishop Dr. John Njoroge Mburu, who heads the advisory Apostolic Council, the preaching life change ministry Church is governed by a National governing Council currently headed by Bishop Dr. John Njoroge. All other Churches that shall be established thereafter shall be governed by a Local Church Council (LCC), constituted by a 5-member board of governors, chaired by the local church pastor who shall be appointed and ordained by the Bishop. The Local Church Council (LCC) , shall be the topmost leadership organ of the church . The LCC reports to the headquarters in Sikhendu near Chalicha junction Kenya. Other leadership organs include Men, Ladies and Youth Fellowships, Children and Teens Ministries, Music, Evangelistic and Hospitality Teams, Home Bible, Ushering and Development Committees. Group leadership include men and ladies groups who also have Rescue Teams to follow up stray/absent/missing members, while Youth Fellowship have Nyumba Kumi groups for communication and accountability.
Spiritual Programs
The typical worship activities and programs consist of; Sunday Services, Youth Sunday Service, Sunday School and Teenagers Services, Sunday New Believers (Discipleship) Classes, Monday Evening Meetings, Tuesday Interdenominational Pastors Prayer meeting, Tuesday Evening Choir Practice, Wednesday Evening Bible Study Meeting, Thursday Evening Home Bible Church (Home Cells) Fellowships, Friday Overnight prayers meetings, Week end Evangelistic Crusades, Mens meeting and Youth Empowerment services.
Church projects and community service
In addition to church ministry and evangelism PLCM also engages in a number of projects and community service, some as a pastoral calling and others for small-scale income. The church intends to runs a primary school in the future in supplementing the government’s efforts in providing both pre-school and primary education to the residents. Some of the needy students are provided with sponsorships to complete their education at various secondary schools, while those who have cleared secondary are sponsored to join universities.
